About Me

Welcome to my homepage! I am an astrophysicist studying radio jets in active galactic nuclei (AGN) and their impact on galaxy evolution. My work focuses on understanding how jet-driven feedback influences multi-phase gas in galaxies across cosmic time.

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at the Space Telescope Science Institute (STScI), where I work with Dr. Patrick Ogle to investigate jet feedback using JWST IFU spectroscopy of radio galaxies. My research uses spatially resolved diagnostics to study shocked molecular and ionized gas, outflows, and the energetics of jet–interstellar medium interaction.

Prior to STScI, I worked with Profs. Christopher P. O’Dea and Stefi Baum at the University of Manitoba, as part of the Canadian Initiative for Radio Astronomy Data Analysis (CIRADA) collaboration, contributing to radio continuum processing pipelines and science-ready data products such as VLASS value-added catalogs. I earned my Ph.D. from the National Centre for Radio Astrophysics (NCRA-TIFR), India under the supervision of Prof. Preeti Kharb, where I studied jet-driven outflows, X-shaped radio galaxies, and the relationship between star formation and AGN activity.

I have extensive experience working with radio interferometric data from VLA, VLBA, and GMRT, and with multi-wavelength facilities including JWST, HST, ALMA, VLT, and Chandra to investigate how AGN influence their host galaxies.

For more on my current projects, please visit my research page.
