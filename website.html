About Me

I'm an astrophysicist passionate about studying jets in active galactic nuclei and their impact on host galaxy evolution. My research journey began at India's National Centre for Radio Astrophysics (NCRA-TIFR), where I earned my Ph.D. under the guidance of Prof. Preeti Kharb. I've explored radio outflows, jet-related feedback, and the interplay of star formation and AGN activity.

Working alongside experts in the field such as Profs. Christopher P. O'Dea, Stefi Baum, and Matthew L. Lister during my postdoctoral journey has expanded my perspective and enriched my expertise. I'm currently working as a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Manitoba and the Canadian Initiative for Radio Astronomy Data Analysis (CIRADA) collaboration. I have extensive experience with handling radio continuum data from telescopes including VLA, VLBA and GMRT. In addition I have made use of multiwavelength data from various other telescopes including HST, ALMA, VLT and Chandra to decode the mysteries of jet formation, and the impact on their environtments. I also play a key role in the CIRADA radio continuum group which is responsible for generating science ready data products including VLASS continuum catalogs and other value added products.

For in-depth details on my research, visit my research page. Welcome to my corner of the universe!
